(P14) Longest Collatz Sequence

The following iterative sequence is defined for the set of positive integers:

{n→n/2if n is even (pari)n→3n+1if n is odd (dispari) \begin{cases} n \to n/2 &\text{if $n$ is even (pari)}\\ n \to 3n + 1 &\text{if $n$ is odd (dispari)} \end{cases} {n→n/2n→3n+1​if n is even (pari)if n is odd (dispari)​

Using the rule above and starting with 131313, we generate the following sequence:

13→40→20→10→5→16→8→4→2→1.13 \to 40 \to 20 \to 10 \to 5 \to 16 \to 8 \to 4 \to 2 \to 1.13→40→20→10→5→16→8→4→2→1.

It can be seen that this sequence (starting at 131313 and finishing at 111) contains 101010 terms. Although it has not been proved yet (Collatz Problem), it is thought that all starting numbers finish at 111. Which starting number, under one million, produces the longest chain? <p class="note"><b>NOTE:</b> Once the chain starts the terms are allowed to go above one million.
