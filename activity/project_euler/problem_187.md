<p>A composite is a number containing at least two prime factors. For example, $15 = 3 \times 5$; $9 = 3 \times 3$; $12 = 2 \times 2 \times 3$.</p>

<p>There are ten composites below thirty containing precisely two, not necessarily distinct, prime factors:
$4, 6, 9, 10, 14, 15, 21, 22, 25, 26$.</p>

<p>How many composite integers, $n \lt 10^8$, have precisely two, not necessarily distinct, prime factors?</p>
